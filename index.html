    /02 — Lifeline

    From the server room
    to the agent economy.

    Twenty-two years of staying ahead of the curve — without making a fuss about it. Every milestone below is a system we built, ran, or are running right now.

    2004

    Next-Gen-IT begins

    The practice opens as an independent managed-services shop. First contract: managed IT & helpdesk for a regional manufacturer.

    Managed IT · MSP
    2006

    Server virtualization practice

    We migrate our first client estate to virtualized server infrastructure. Hardware budgets fall by 60% overnight. The practice quickly becomes our largest line of business.

    Virtualization
    2009

    Santa Fe project base

    A desert studio in Santa Fe becomes the base for long-form infrastructure design and 24/7 ops work for clients across the Mountain West.

    Infrastructure consulting
    2011

    Cloud migration services

    AWS goes mainstream and so do we. Next-Gen-IT runs its first full client migration from on-prem to public cloud. Hybrid becomes a permanent fixture of the portfolio.

    Cloud · Hybrid
    2014

    Cybersecurity & SOC practice

    Managed detection & response stands up alongside the NOC. The two teams share a queue from day one — a pattern that returns a decade later in ACP’s event stream.

    SecOps
    2016

    Platform-engineering work moves to the West Coast. Focus: automation, DevOps, and the early plumbing for what would become our agent-orchestration work.

    DevOps · Platform
    2018

    Clients run on AWS, Azure and GCP — sometimes all three. We formalize an SRE practice and build the first internal tooling for cross-cloud cost & reliability.

    2020

    /06 — FAQ

    Common questions.

    Quick answers about how we work, what we build, and how the Academy fits in.

    Are you really independent?

    Yes. Next-Gen-IT is privately held, German-led, and owns Basys Analytics as a wholly-owned subsidiary. No outside capital, no obligation to ship features we don’t believe in.

    What kind of engagements do you take?

    Long-running managed-services contracts, infrastructure modernisation projects, and ACP pilots. We don’t do one-off tickets, body-shop staffing, or “AI strategy” slide decks.

    Where do you operate?

    Engineering happens between Stuttgart, Santa Fe, and San Francisco. Clients are global; on-site work is most common across the DACH region and the western US.

    How does the Academy actually work?

    Free Learning Apps ship through the Basys marketplace today. The Academy itself — structured tracks, mentor office hours, sponsored seats funded by marketplace revenue — is being rolled out. See the education roadmap.

    Do you take on agent / A2A pilots?

    Yes — ACP is in internal pilot now. Allowlisted partners can request access; the rest is open via the marketplace as we harden the workspace.
